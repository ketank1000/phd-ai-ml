# 3. Research Design

> **Part A — Research Methodology** | SIU PET Study Guide

---

- [ ] **Types of Research Design**

  | Design | Description | When to Use |
  |--------|-------------|------------|
  | **Experimental** | Manipulates independent variable, measures effect | Causal relationships |
  | **Quasi-experimental** | Like experimental but without full randomization | Field studies |
  | **Non-experimental** | No manipulation of variables | Observational studies |
  | **Descriptive** | Describes what exists | Surveys, case studies |
  | **Correlational** | Measures relationship between variables | Association studies |
  | **Longitudinal** | Data collected over time | Trend analysis |
  | **Cross-sectional** | Data at one point in time | Snapshot analysis |

- [ ] **Variables**
  - **Independent variable (IV)**: The variable you manipulate (e.g., learning rate)
  - **Dependent variable (DV)**: The variable you measure (e.g., model accuracy)
  - **Control variable**: Kept constant to isolate IV's effect
  - **Confounding variable**: Unwanted variable affecting DV
  - **Moderating variable**: Affects the strength of the IV-DV relationship
  - **Mediating variable**: Explains the mechanism between IV and DV

- [ ] **Validity**
  - **Internal validity**: Are the results due to the IV, not confounders?
  - **External validity**: Can results be generalized?
  - **Construct validity**: Does the test measure the intended concept?
  - **Content validity**: Does the test cover the full domain?
  - **Face validity**: Does it appear to measure what it claims?

- [ ] **Reliability**
  - **Test-retest**: Consistency over time
  - **Inter-rater**: Agreement between observers
  - **Internal consistency**: Cronbach's alpha (α > 0.7 is acceptable)
  - **Split-half**: Correlation between two halves of a test

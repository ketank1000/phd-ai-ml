# 4. Sampling Techniques

> **Part A — Research Methodology** | SIU PET Study Guide

---

- [ ] **Population vs. Sample**
  - Population: Entire group of interest
  - Sample: Subset selected for study
  - Sampling frame: List of all elements in the population

- [ ] **Probability Sampling** (each element has a known, non-zero chance)

  | Method | How It Works | Pros/Cons |
  |--------|-------------|-----------|
  | **Simple Random** | Each element equally likely (lottery, random number) | Unbiased; needs complete list |
  | **Stratified** | Divide into strata, random sample from each | Ensures representation of subgroups |
  | **Cluster** | Divide into clusters, randomly select clusters | Cost-effective; higher sampling error |
  | **Systematic** | Every kth element (k = N/n) | Easy; risky if periodic pattern exists |

- [ ] **Non-Probability Sampling**

  | Method | How It Works | Pros/Cons |
  |--------|-------------|-----------|
  | **Convenience** | Whoever is available | Easy; highly biased |
  | **Purposive/Judgmental** | Researcher's judgment | Good for specific cases; subjective |
  | **Snowball** | Existing subjects recruit future subjects | Good for hidden populations |
  | **Quota** | Non-random selection to match population proportions | Ensures proportional representation; biased |

- [ ] **Sample Size Determination**
  - Factors: desired confidence level, margin of error, population variability
  - Formula: $n = \frac{Z^2 \cdot p \cdot (1-p)}{e^2}$ where Z = z-score, p = population proportion, e = margin of error
  - Rule of thumb: larger sample → more reliable results

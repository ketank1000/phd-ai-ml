# 2. Research Problem & Literature Review

> **Part A — Research Methodology** | SIU PET Study Guide

---

- [ ] **Identification of Research Problem**
  - Sources: personal experience, academic literature, prior research gaps, practical issues
  - Criteria: feasibility, novelty, ethical considerations, significance

- [ ] **Formulation of Research Problem**
  - Statement of the problem (clear, concise)
  - Scope and limitations
  - Research questions vs. research objectives

- [ ] **Literature Review**
  - **Purpose**: Identify gaps, avoid duplication, build theoretical framework
  - **Sources**:
    - Primary: research papers, theses, conference proceedings
    - Secondary: textbooks, review articles, handbooks
    - Tertiary: encyclopedias, indexes, abstracts
  - **Databases**: IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, Google Scholar, Scopus, Web of Science
  - **Techniques**: Systematic review, meta-analysis, bibliometric analysis

- [ ] **Hypothesis**
  - **Definition**: A tentative statement about the relationship between variables
  - **Types**:
    - Null hypothesis (H₀): No significant difference/effect
    - Alternative hypothesis (H₁ or Hₐ): Significant difference/effect exists
    - Directional vs. non-directional
  - **Characteristics**: Testable, falsifiable, specific, clear
  - **Testing**: Accept/reject based on statistical evidence
